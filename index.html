The Evidence Packaging Problem

CMMC readiness teams lose weeks turning scattered screenshots, exports, and policy artifacts into evidence that is reusable, reviewable, and ready for assessor handoff. In disconnected or tightly controlled environments, cloud-first GRC workflows add friction instead of removing it.

What Hardseal Does

Hardseal collects evidence locally, packages it consistently, and generates assessor-ready outputs — without depending on cloud connectivity.

Offline Evidence Capture

Collect compliance evidence directly from disconnected and offline systems without requiring cloud connectivity. Evidence stays on your infrastructure.

Ed25519 Signing

Cryptographically sign all evidence with deterministic algorithms. Exactly what FedRAMP's machine-readable mandate demands—no AI-generated narratives.

OSCAL v1.1.2 Export

Generate machine-readable compliance profiles in OSCAL format, ready for FedRAMP and automated assessment workflows.

SSP & POA&M Auto-Generation

Automatically generate System Security Plans and Plans of Action & Milestones from collected evidence and control mappings.

Self-Hosted & Reproducible

Deploy Hardseal on your own infrastructure. Every assessment result is reproducible, auditable, and assessor-ready.

Cross-Platform Support

Works on Windows, Linux, and macOS. Collect evidence from legacy systems, containerized environments, and everything in between.

How It Works

Three steps from scattered evidence to assessor-ready compliance packages.

1

Collect

Deploy Hardseal on isolated systems. Automatically gather evidence from logs, policies, configurations, and audit trails. No cloud required.

2

Sign

Evidence is cryptographically signed with Ed25519. Create deterministic packages that prove integrity and reproducibility to assessors.

3

Export

Generate OSCAL-compliant exports, SSPs, POA&Ms, and assessor-ready compliance packages in seconds. Deploy to any environment.

Cryptographic Integrity

Ed25519 signing with deterministic key generation. Sign, verify, tamper-reject, and key rotation all tested. Private keys stay under your control.

OSCAL v1.1.2 Schema Validated

Component-definition output validated against NIST OSCAL schema. UUIDs, metadata, roles, parties, and back-matter resources all conformant.

Full Control Coverage

All 14 CMMC Level 2 families (AC, AU, AT, CM, IA, IR, MA, MP, PS, PE, RA, CA, SC, SI) with all 110 controls defined and evidence-mapped.

Honest Scope

53 controls fully automated. 29 partially automated. 28 require manual input (policy and process decisions). This is an accelerator, not a replacement.

OSCAL Validation Scope

Our OSCAL component-definition output passes 22/22 structural validation checks: valid UUIDs (RFC 4122), required metadata fields, roles/parties linkage, control-implementation structure, back-matter resource integrity, and namespace conformance to NIST OSCAL v1.1.2 schema. This covers CMMC Level 2 (NIST 800-171 Rev 2) controls — not FedRAMP-specific extensions.

Package Integrity

SHA-256 (all 14 modules): a85ee941...a71595 — Zero external dependencies. Pure Python stdlib. Verify before deployment in air-gapped environments.
